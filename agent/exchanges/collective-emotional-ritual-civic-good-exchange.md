## The idea in one paragraph

The steward's instinct — *feeling and emotion may be a key piece of the project and of human flourishing; not everything needs to be a falsifiable rational claim* — sharpens into a precise contribution once you split emotion's **two jobs**. As **evidence** ("I feel meaning, therefore the mechanism works"), felt experience is the worst possible readout and must be firewalled. As a **constituent good** ("this was spiritual"), feeling is not a measurement of a good — it *is* the good, and subjecting it to falsification is a category error; the discipline there is *cite, don't falsify*. Collective emotional ritual (a World Cup, a parade, a pub full of fans) is the paradigm case of the second job — and the corpus, which has a rich theory of the first, has almost no theory of the second. But the same episode that names the good also names the danger: collective feeling is **the most harvestable thing humans have** (sportwashing; the 1978 Argentina junta World Cup, where collective joy and state terror ran on one instrument simultaneously). So the move is Tariq Panja's *"carry both thoughts"*: honor the feeling as a good **and** hold the institutions that harvest it to account. The genuinely testable question is whether a given arrangement of collective ritual **channels into** civic agency or **drains** it — and whether that question even has a portable answer, or dissolves case-by-case, is the gate this exchange opens on.

| **E33-C1** | **Two registers for collective feeling.** Collective emotional ritual plays two *separable* roles — feeling-as-evidence (epistemically suspect; firewall it) and feeling-as-constituent-good (a constituent of a flourishing civic life; cite, don't falsify it). The project theorizes the first and has barely named the second. | Shown false if no usable distinction exists in practice — i.e., every real instance of collective feeling carries both roles inseparably ([feeling riff OQ#2](../explorations/feeling-as-a-first-order-civic-good-riff.md#9-open-questions)), so "cite the good, firewall the evidence" cannot be operationalized on actual cases. (Note: this is the same *separability* move that took a BLOCKING hit in [#32 C2](decorrelate-to-see-correlate-to-belong-exchange.md#round-1--the-falsifiable-claim-set-e32-c1--c5) — it is on trial here, not assumed.) |
| **E33-C2** | **The intrinsic-good claim is value-contested (cite, don't adopt).** Whether communal joy / collective effervescence is *intrinsically* a first-order civic good is a [flavor-(c) value-contested](../explorations/verifiers-for-reality-riff.md#7-three-flavors-of-no-verifier) claim — the project should cite it across traditions, not adopt it as a principle. | Shown false if the claim is actually empirically decidable (then it is not flavor-c and must be tested, not merely cited), **or** if it is incoherent (no determinate content to cite). |
| **E33-C3** | **The channeling claim is the falsifiable edge — and it is portable (THE GATE).** The testable claim is *structural*: whether a given arrangement of collective ritual **channels into** civic agency (participation, solidarity that outlasts the event, cross-cleavage coexistence) or **drains** it (depoliticizing distraction / bread-and-circuses) — and this admits a *portable* predictor, not only case-by-case verdicts. | Shown false if channel-vs-drain dissolves into irreducibly case-specific outcomes (1978 Argentina vs. Stewart's "Hungary tossing out Orbán") with **no** portable predictor — i.e., [feeling riff OQ#4](../explorations/feeling-as-a-first-order-civic-good-riff.md#9-open-questions) resolves negative. If so, §6 stays a riff and does not become a testable program. |
| **E33-C4** | **The harvest discipline is mandatory and partly falsifiable ("carry both thoughts").** Collective feeling is the most harvestable human good (sportwashing; 1978 Argentina; the [demand-side meaning-deficit theory of misinformation, #30](demand-side-misinformation-meaning-deficit-exchange.md)). Honoring it as a good does **not** exempt the institutions that harvest it; and *whether an arrangement tends to manufacture vs. enable authentic collective feeling is itself a partly observable, structural question*. | Shown false if (a) manufacture-vs-enable is not even partly observable (the discipline has no teeth), **or** (b) protecting authentic feeling and holding harvesters accountable cannot be jointly designed — the two thoughts cannot both be carried in one institution. |

Disposition ceiling: a candidate note / exchange finding, only after cross-lineage review and **gated on C3**. | Falsified if the structural-protection claim is unbuildable, or licenses over-reach into private life (the [Brooks](../../sources/source-modern-wisdom-1109-brooks-digest.md) anti-structural trap *inverted* — the state curating feeling), or collapses back into the intrinsic claim (then it is value-contested, cite-only, and adds nothing to #29). |
