# Develop-Leg Test-Design Memo — Proposed v0.3 Divergence Resolutions

> [Pipeline Run 001](discovery-principle-develop-leg-pipeline-run-001.md) folded its five BLOCKING fixes into memo [v0.2](discovery-principle-develop-leg-test-design-memo.md) but left **three reviewer divergences open** ([Run 001 §5.2](discovery-principle-develop-leg-pipeline-run-001.md)) — points where the reviewers disagreed *with each other* and which the synthesizer deliberately did **not** majority-vote. This document proposes a resolution for each, for **steward sign-off**. On sign-off they fold into memo v0.3. **Nothing here is promoted; the [Exchange #25 hold](shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md) governs; this is non-evidence for S17.**
>
> **Resolution principle used throughout:** where two reviewers compete for the *same lever*, the resolution **splits the lever** so each constraint gets its own instrument, rather than trading one off against the other. (This is the same move v0.2 used to dissolve the B1 F3↔ethics BLOCKING via demote + F3a/F3b.)

---

## Divergence 1 — F3 catch-22 (falsifiability vs. ethics)

**The conflict ([Run 001 §5.2](discovery-principle-develop-leg-pipeline-run-001.md)).** The adversary held that F3 ("the effect needs manufactured crises") can't trip — it is un-falsifiable as written; the ethics monitor held the opposite worry, that the design must *enforce* a manufactured-crisis ban. The two pull on the same lever (what counts as a qualifying shock), so v0.2's demote-and-split (F3a/F3b) needs finalizing.

**Proposed resolution — finalize the split, assign each half its own instrument.**

- **F3a (the falsifier, kept):** the repair effect must appear under **naturally-occurring shocks of pre-registered magnitude that are observed, never induced.** If no natural-shock practice body out-repairs its matched control, the hypothesis is weakened — so F3a *can* return NO (answers the adversary).
- **F3b (a bright-line exclusion, not a falsifier):** the study **never induces, nudges, manufactures, or times** a crisis; any body whose break appears manufactured or nudged is **excluded, not harvested** (answers the ethics monitor). F3b is enforced by the ethics monitor with **STOP authority**, not scored.

The catch-22 dissolves because falsifiability now rides on F3a (natural shocks) while the ethics constraint rides on F3b (no manufacture) — they no longer compete for the same lever. **Cost:** F3a needs a pre-registered, quantitative natural-shock magnitude criterion (already a Run 001 MAJOR item) so "of sufficient magnitude" is not a researcher degree of freedom.

## Divergence 2 — rubric rigidity vs. community veto

**The conflict.** The measurement skeptic wants a **rigid, pre-registered** repair-capacity rubric (to block objective-washing / researcher degrees of freedom — Run 001 B2); the ethics monitor wants a **STOP** when a community contests its own classification (their "calcification" may be their "principled solidarity").

**Proposed resolution — freeze the rubric for everyone; quarantine (do not rescore) a contested case.**

- The rubric and its scoring are **frozen at pre-registration** and **never edited mid-study** (answers the skeptic — no post-hoc rubric drift, the same rubric applies to every body).
- When a community **contests its classification**, the contest is entered in a **community-contest log** and triggers two things: **(i)** scoring of *that body* is **STOPPED** — its data is **set aside, not forced through the contested rubric**; **(ii)** the contest is carried to analysis as **evidence about rubric validity**, not as a license to rescore to taste.

So the rubric stays rigid (no drift, no objective-washing) **and** a contested classification can never become a finding. The affected community holds a veto over *being scored by a rubric it rejects* — but not a lever to *edit the rubric*. This honors the "principled solidarity" worry without reopening the researcher-degrees-of-freedom hole. **Pre-register** the contest threshold (what counts as a community contesting) so the STOP itself is not a degree of freedom.

## Divergence 3 — unit of consent vs. unit of analysis

**The conflict.** The unit of *analysis* is the **body/process** (accepted by skeptic + adversary; affirmed as necessary in Run 001 §5.3). The ethics monitor calls leader opt-in a **"consent illusion"** and demands **individual** consent — which could make the matched comparison impractical.

**Proposed resolution — decouple the unit of analysis from the unit of consent; layer consent by data type.**

- **Unit of analysis stays the body/process** (unchanged — it is necessary, per the AFFIRMING finding).
- **Unit of consent is layered:**
  - **Individual informed consent is required for any individual-level data** — interviews, individual instruments, anything attributable to a person. No exceptions.
  - **For behavioral observation of a body's *already-public* deliberative process,** organizational/leader consent **plus an individual opt-out** (clear notice + a right to withdraw one's own contributions from the analysis) substitutes for individual opt-in. This keeps the comparison practical without the consent illusion, because no individual is studied *as an individual* without their opt-in, and anyone may remove themselves from the public-process record.
  - **Where a body's process is not public,** fall back to **individual opt-in** (the public-process exception does not apply).

This is the standard organizational-research resolution: individual opt-in for individual data; organizational consent + individual opt-out for observation of public collective process. **Document the public/non-public boundary explicitly** in the protocol, and have the ethics monitor confirm the opt-out is real (reachable, low-friction, honored).
