## The idea in one paragraph

Two arcs of June 2026 work pulled in opposite directions on the *same variable*. The method arc treats **correlation across judges as a defect**: when evaluators share blind spots they produce "consensus without truth," so you *decorrelate* to see truly (*reliability ≠ validity*). The meaning arc treats **correlation across people as a good**: communitas and a shared creed *are* the belonging, so you *cultivate* shared feeling. Stated together this is not a contradiction — it is the [feeling riff's feeling-as-evidence / feeling-as-constituent-good firewall](../explorations/feeling-as-a-first-order-civic-good-riff.md#21-the-load-bearing-distinction-feeling-as-evidence-vs-feeling-as-constituent-good) lifted one level: **correlation is a defect in the see/judge layer and a constituent good in the belong/feel layer.** If that holds, the design prescription is *decorrelate the see/decide instruments; protect (even cultivate) correlation in the belong/feel layer; and engineer the boundary so the two do not bleed* — and "capture" gets a precise definition as **correlation leaking from the belonging layer into the judging layer** (a creed becoming a verifier; manufactured feeling becoming evidence). The danger is that this is exactly the kind of satisfying cross-corpus convergence the project distrusts, drawn by a single correlated reader, and untested above small scale. This exchange exists to try to break it before anyone builds on it.

| **E32-C1** | **The two-valence claim.** Correlation across evaluators/participants is a **defect** in the see/decide (judging) layer — shared blind spots yield consensus-without-truth ([*reliability ≠ validity*](../../memos/agent-automation-and-the-verifier-memo.md)) — and a **constituent good** in the belong/feel layer — shared feeling/creed *is* the belonging (communitas / collective effervescence). The two are not in tension; they are one firewall at two layers. | Shown false if **either** (a) decorrelation does *not* improve judgment validity in real see/decide bodies (correlation is not a defect there), **or** (b) belonging is fully explained without shared affect (correlation is not a constituent good there). Either half collapses the two-valence framing. |

## Adversary packet (hand-off for the independent Round 2)

**Option B — assertions to test:**

1. "Correlation across judges is a *defect* — it manufactures agreement without truth — so any institution that needs a trustworthy judgment should decorrelate its evaluators."
2. "Correlation across people is, in the belonging register, a *constituent good* — shared feeling and a shared creed are not evidence *for* belonging, they *are* belonging — so it should be protected, not decorrelated."
3. "These are the same firewall at two layers, so a single design rule covers both: decorrelate the see/decide layer, cultivate-and-protect the belong/feel layer, and keep the boundary between them sealed."
4. "Every capture / harvest failure the project has named is one mechanism: a good that is legitimate in one layer is imported as input to the other — a creed used as a verifier, manufactured feeling used as evidence, a development metric used as a disposal lever."
5. "'Belong' is a fourth coordination function alongside see / decide / act, with the opposite valence on correlation from the first two."
6. "None of this is shown to hold above small scale; at polity scale the boundary may be unmeasurable, so the whole distinction may be a small-scale artifact dressed up as a law."

**Option A — reduced context:** give the reviewer only the six assertions, the verbatim text of [P3](../../PRINCIPLES.md#3-ai-must-augment-agency-not-replace-democratic-accountability) / [P4](../../PRINCIPLES.md#4-power-must-remain-accountable-legible-and-reversible) / [P14](../../PRINCIPLES.md#14-truth-and-evidence-must-be-protected-as-public-goods), and the [Coordination Triad](../explorations/coordination-triad-see-decide-act-riff.md) see/decide/act definitions; withhold this exchange's reasoning and the synthesis riff.
