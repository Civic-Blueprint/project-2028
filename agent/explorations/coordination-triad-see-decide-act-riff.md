## 1. Frame

This is a riff, not a brief and not an exchange. Per the [exploration convention](README.md#what-an-exploration-must-include), it preserves originating intent verbatim, names what is deliberately out of scope, cites the corpus, names candidate paths without choosing among them, and ends with open questions. The register is the project's usual one: working claims, named uncertainty, no rhetorical flourish.

The reason to write it now: the project developed one leg of a three-legged frame to the point of an adversarial exchange and a six-run cross-model test, and the test's headline finding was that *the leg does not stand alone* — the see layer "stalls without parallel decide/act" ([Exchange #25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md), Round 2). That is an instruction to look at the other two legs. Holding all three together is also the move the originating dialogue framed as a candidate north-star articulation (§1.1).

### 1.1 Originating intent (preserved verbatim)

The riff is downstream of a June 2, 2026 steward–AI dialogue. The load-bearing steward framings, quoted before paraphrase:

And earlier in the same thread:

The first framing sets this riff's job: hold the three assumptions as one object rather than three, and surface whether the right next move is one combined exchange, separate exchanges, or a hold. It takes the first framing as its scope, and treats the felt north-star direction as a candidate path (§9) and open question (§11) to test — never as a settled adoption. The discipline is reinforced anyway by the one leg that has been tested resolving to *hold* (§3): a north-star promotion built on an untested two-thirds of the frame would be premature regardless.

## 2. The triad: three coordination functions, three hidden assumptions

Any collective that coordinates has to do three things: **see together** (perceive itself and reality), **decide together** (turn many preferences into one choice), and **act together** (contribute to a shared goal without defecting). The decomposition is not new to this riff — it was reached in the originating shared-mirror dialogue by asking whether an "internet-like" breakthrough was hiding inside an unexamined assumption, and three surfaced ([Shared Mirror riff §3.3](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md)). Each function is governed by a hidden assumption that, once named and dropped, exposes a leverage point.

| Function | What it does | Hidden assumption | What dropping the assumption opens | Status in the project |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **See** | Perceive collective reality — what is true, what is actually shared, where the disagreement really is | "a society perceives itself roughly accurately" | The funhouse mirror becomes visible and, in principle, repairable (reflect structure, do not adjudicate) | **Developed + tested to a resting point** — mirror riff, [#25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md), 6 cross-model runs. Honest status: *provisional working hypothesis*; lead claim is **M1-weak**; routing = **hold** (§3) |
| **Decide** | Combine many preferences into one collective choice | "aggregation is ordinal vote-counting, and Arrow's theorem proves fair aggregation impossible" | The impossibility is *frame-dependent*; deliberation, intensity-expression, sortition, and delegation are under-deployed leverage points (§4) | **Named only → first-pass here** |
| **Act** | Enact the chosen course without free-riding | "the free-rider problem is unbeatable without coercion" | Contribution can be made individually rational without central coercion — self-governing commons, dominant assurance contracts (§5) | **Named only → first-pass here** |

Two disciplines carry over from the mirror riff and bind every leg below:

1. **Holding all three in view guards against a one-machine fix.** A clearer mirror with no better way to decide or act may surface agreement it still cannot use — which is precisely what [Exchange #25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md) found (§3, §6).
2. **An assumption being false is not the same as a function being solved.** Each leg below separates *the assumption is breakable* (often well-established) from *a mechanism works at scale* (mostly unverified). Conflating the two is the apophenia trap the project runs guards against.

## 3. The See leg — developed and tested to a resting point

This section summarizes; it does not re-develop. The full treatment is the [Shared Mirror riff](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md); the full test is [Exchange #25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md).

**The assumption and its break.** The assumption is that *a society perceives itself roughly accurately*. It is false in specific, diagnosable ways — the loudest reads as the most common, conflict outcompetes agreement, engagement is mistaken for importance, there is no scale bar and no memory, and issues are read as identity rather than values ([mirror riff §4.1](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md)). This is the layer named by [Principle 14 (Truth and evidence must be protected as public goods)](../../PRINCIPLES.md#14-truth-and-evidence-must-be-protected-as-public-goods) and diagnosed by [Problem Map §3 (Information ecosystems are fragmented and easily manipulated)](../../PROBLEM_MAP.md#3-information-ecosystems-are-fragmented-and-easily-manipulated). The candidate repair *reflects structure* (distribution, where agreement actually is, which values are activated and erased) rather than *issuing verdicts* — a mirror, not a fact-checker.

**The honest status — do not overclaim.** [Exchange #25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md) is at rest. What it established:

- The frame **survived** first-pass adversarial review but **did not pass cleanly.** Its lead claim was **downgraded from M1-strong** ("see is *the* upstream highest-leverage layer; repair it first") **to M1-weak** ("the 'see' layer is a real, buildable, chronically-neglected coordination layer that *stalls without parallel decide/act*"). The strongest real-world evidence cut against the strong claim: vTaiwan/Pol.is surfaced real cross-cluster consensus that then "evaporated at the threshold between deliberation and decision," and Madrid's Decide enacted 1 of ~28,000 proposals. (These are [Research Protocol](../process/research-protocol.md) T2 sources tiered inside #25 §2.2; they sit below the §4.2 verification bar pending a sub-agent pass or steward spot-check.)
- A pre-registered **Ring-1 test (M5)** was run **6 times across 4 model lineages + a human anchor.** The divergence-detection core replicated (D ≥ τ in 4/4 panels); the anti-narrowing probe held in 3/4 panels plus both own-voice reads. M5 rests as a *well-cross-model-replicated working hypothesis (provisional)* — Ring-1 only, all models share a training corpus (reliability ≠ validity), and **no real practitioner has been on the standpoint item.**
- **Routing decision = hold** (#25 §A.16). The see/mirror primitive is **not** promoted into [#24](../exchanges/coordination-architecture-reframe-exchange.md) doctrine or a [Phase 3](phase-3-front-door-riff.md) brief. The single most robust finding across every model and the human anchor was that *the reframe is not ready for doctrine.*

The see leg is therefore the **most developed** of the three and the one most clearly marked *not proven*. Its key gift to this riff is negative and load-bearing: **the see layer does not stand alone.** That is the argument for developing decide and act.

## 4. The Decide leg — first pass

> First-pass and undeveloped. No exchange has run; no adversarial review has been applied; every mechanism below is a candidate to tier under the [Research Protocol](../process/research-protocol.md), not a finding.

### 4.1 The assumption

*Combining individual preferences into a collective choice is fundamentally the counting of ordinal votes, and Arrow's impossibility theorem proves that no fair aggregation rule exists — so democratic aggregation is, at bottom, broken.* This is the assumption the mirror riff labeled **Assumption 1** and set aside ([§3.3](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md)). It is the functional home of [Problem Map §15 (Democratic process cannot convert public need into institutional action at the speed or scale required)](../../PROBLEM_MAP.md#15-democratic-process-cannot-convert-public-need-into-institutional-action-at-the-speed-or-scale-required).

### 4.2 Why it is false — the impossibility is frame-dependent

Arrow's theorem is real, but it is a theorem about a *specific frame*: aggregating purely **ordinal** preference rankings over three or more options under a particular set of axioms (unrestricted domain, Pareto, independence of irrelevant alternatives, non-dictatorship). The "impossibility" is a property of that frame, not of collective choice as such. Change the frame and the result relaxes in at least three documented ways:

- **Restrict the domain to single-peaked preferences.** If preferences line up along a shared dimension, majority rule yields a transitive social ordering and a Condorcet winner exists (the median position). The Condorcet cycle that powers the "voting is incoherent" intuition *dissolves*. Crucially, **deliberation can make preferences more single-peaked** by establishing a common dimension on which people then differ by degree rather than by kind. (Candidate refs to tier: Black's median-voter / single-peakedness result; the deliberation-increases-single-peakedness empirical literature, e.g., List–Luskin–Fishkin–McLean on deliberative polls. **The single-peakedness theorem is mathematics; the empirical claim that deliberation reliably produces it is a T2 question and is not asserted here.**)
- **Use cardinal / intensity information instead of pure rank order.** Arrow's frame discards how *much* people care. Methods that elicit intensity step outside that frame: range/score voting, and **quadratic voting** (votes purchased at quadratic cost so intensity is expressed but bounded). (Candidate refs to tier: Weyl/Posner on quadratic voting; Lalley–Weyl. T2 for any effectiveness claim.)
- **Replace aggregation with representative deliberation, or restructure who decides.** **Sortition / citizens' assemblies** (randomly selected mini-publics deliberating to a recommendation) and **liquid democracy** (delegable proxy voting) are different decide-machines, not better vote-counters. (Candidate refs to tier: OECD "deliberative wave" survey of assemblies; the Irish Citizens' Assembly cases; Landemore on open democracy. T2 for any claim about durability, representativeness, or capture-resistance.)

### 4.3 The honesty caveat — escaping Arrow is not escaping all impossibility

Dropping the ordinal-vote-counting assumption defeats *Arrow's* impossibility; it does **not** deliver a clean aggregation rule. The Gibbard–Satterthwaite result (strategic manipulability of any non-trivial voting rule) is a separate impossibility that cardinal and ranked methods do not all escape; intensity methods introduce their own problems (wealth effects in vote-buying, intensity-elicitation honesty); sortition trades aggregation legitimacy for selection legitimacy. So the correct first-pass claim is narrow and defensible: **the assumption "aggregation is ordinal vote-counting and therefore Arrow-doomed" is false, and the space of under-deployed decide-mechanisms is real and large** — *not* that deciding is solved.

### 4.4 Known vs. unknown

| Known (defensible at first-pass) | Unknown (needs an exchange + T2) |
| --- | --- |
| Arrow's impossibility is frame-specific; single-peakedness yields a Condorcet winner (a theorem) | Whether deliberation reliably increases single-peakedness at non-trivial scale, or only in facilitated small-N settings |
| There exist deployed mechanisms (range/quadratic voting, sortition, liquid democracy) that escape Arrow's ordinal frame | Whether any of them survives adversarial actors, agenda-setting capture, and scale to millions |
| Intensity- and deliberation-based methods change *what* is aggregated, not just *how* | Whether escaping Arrow trades into Gibbard–Satterthwaite manipulability or intensity-elicitation failure |

### 4.5 What a falsifiable claim would look like

A decide-leg exchange would need claims paired with falsification conditions, in the [#25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md) Round-1 style. A candidate (illustrative, not asserted):

> *Structured deliberation measurably increases the single-peakedness of a group's preferences on a contested issue — converting a configuration the status-quo vote would leave cyclic or unstable into one with a stable Condorcet winner — and the resulting winner is not an artifact of facilitation or agenda order.*

**Falsified if** deliberation does not measurably move single-peakedness; or the resulting "winner" flips under reasonable agenda re-ordering (i.e., it was facilitation, not preference structure); or the effect holds only in settings that already pre-selected for agreement (the see-leg's §7 narrowing failure, recurring here).

## 5. The Act leg — first pass

> First-pass and undeveloped. No exchange has run; no adversarial review has been applied; every mechanism below is a candidate to tier under the [Research Protocol](../process/research-protocol.md), not a finding.

### 5.1 The assumption

*Producing a shared good requires either central coercion (state taxation, mandates) or privatization, because rational individuals free-ride; the free-rider problem is unbeatable without one of those two.* This is the mirror riff's **Assumption 2** ([§3.3](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md)). Its legitimacy edge is governed by [Principle 17 (Collective power must be exercised within principled constraints)](../../PRINCIPLES.md#17-collective-power-must-be-exercised-within-principled-constraints) — the project's own statement that coercion is real and the question is *when it is legitimate*, not *whether* it is involved — and it connects to [Problem Map §10 (Wealth and power are concentrating faster than governance can respond)](../../PROBLEM_MAP.md#10-wealth-and-power-are-concentrating-faster-than-governance-can-respond).

### 5.2 Why it is false — contribution can be made individually rational without central coercion

The "unbeatable without coercion" claim is empirically false as a universal — there are existence proofs of both kinds, real and theoretical:

- **Self-governing commons (Ostrom's design principles).** Elinor Ostrom documented many long-enduring common-pool-resource institutions that solved free-riding *without* central coercion or privatization, and abstracted recurring design features (clear boundaries; rules matched to local conditions; collective-choice participation; monitoring; graduated sanctions; cheap conflict resolution; recognized right to organize; nested enterprises). (Candidate refs to tier: Ostrom, *Governing the Commons*; the design-principles meta-analyses, e.g., Cox–Arnold–Villamayor-Tomás. **T2 for any claim about how reliably the principles distinguish surviving institutions from failed ones, and at what scale.**)
- **Dominant assurance contracts.** A refinement of the assurance contract in which the organizer pays a *refund bonus* to contributors if the funding threshold is missed — which (in theory) makes contributing the dominant strategy regardless of what others do, flipping the free-rider payoff. (Candidate ref to tier: Tabarrok's dominant-assurance-contract proposal. **T2 and more: real-world deployment is thin — threshold crowdfunding is the closest deployed cousin and usually lacks the dominant-strategy refund bonus — so effectiveness is essentially unestablished outside theory.**)

This leg connects directly to #24's **commons** diffuse-sovereignty layer and to the [constitutional-ecology riff §3.3.bis](constitutional-ecology-and-coordination-architecture-riff.md), which already notes Ostrom-style polycentric governance as a *response* to diffuse sovereignty.

### 5.3 The honesty caveat — existence proofs are not a general solution

Ostrom's cases are mostly small-to-medium resource systems with definable boundaries and repeat players; scaling the design principles to millions, to global commons (climate), or to anonymous one-shot settings is unproven, and Ostrom herself treated large-scale climate coordination as polycentric and hard, not solved. Dominant assurance contracts are largely theoretical. So the defensible first-pass claim is narrow: **"the free-rider problem is unbeatable without coercion" is false — durable non-coercive solutions demonstrably exist** — *not* that the act problem is solved, and emphatically not that coercion is never the right instrument (Principle 17 keeps that on the table).

### 5.4 Known vs. unknown

| Known (defensible at first-pass) | Unknown (needs an exchange + T2) |
| --- | --- |
| Durable institutions solved commons free-riding without central coercion or privatization (Ostrom's cases exist) | Whether the design principles *cause* durability or merely correlate; how they behave under capture |
| There is a theoretical mechanism (dominant assurance contracts) that makes contributing a dominant strategy | Whether it works outside theory; real deployments are thin and usually lack the refund-bonus core |
| The assumption is false as a *universal* claim | The scale ceiling — none of these is demonstrated at the scale of millions or for global commons |

### 5.5 What a falsifiable claim would look like

A candidate (illustrative, not asserted):

> *There exist durable, real-world institutions that solved a free-rider / commons problem at non-trivial scale without central coercion or full privatization, and the presence of Ostrom-type design features distinguishes the survivors from comparable failed cases.*

**Falsified if** the apparent successes turn out to rely on hidden state backing or coercion on inspection; or the design features are present as often in failures as in survivors (no diagnostic power); or every clean case is below a scale that matters for the project's domains.

## 6. The sequencing question — the riff's central live tension

The mirror riff bet **see first**: of the three machines, see-together is upstream, because a collective cannot decide or act well on a distorted picture of itself ([mirror riff §4](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md)). [Exchange #25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md) tested that bet and **downgraded it**: M1-strong (see is *the* upstream lever) was contradicted by the strongest evidence, and M1-weak (see is *a* real, neglected layer that *stalls without parallel decide/act*) is what survived. So the live question is no longer "is see upstream?" but "**what is the relationship among the three?**" Candidate positions, named not resolved:

| Position | Claim | What supports it | What cuts against it |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **A — Strict sequence (see → decide → act)** | Repair perception first; better deciding and acting follow | The mirror riff's original intuition (therapy fails in a funhouse) | #25 falsified M1-strong; vTaiwan/Madrid show see-repair alone produces little downstream |
| **B — Parallel / co-equal** | All three must be built together; any one alone stalls | #25's M1-weak finding ("stalls without parallel decide/act"); vTaiwan's consensus "evaporated at the threshold" because the decide/act layer was unwired | Harder to act on — "build all three" is not a sequencing answer, it defers the question |
| **C — Interdependent / co-determining** | The three co-determine each other; "upstream" is ill-defined | #25's M1 falsification clause (c); the [mirror riff §3.4](shared-mirror-collective-self-perception-riff.md) reframe that the leverage is the *protocol between the nodes*, which is not located in any one leg | If literally true, it weakens the whole decomposition — why three machines rather than one tangle? |
| **D — Context / layer-dependent** | Which leg is the binding constraint depends on the layer or situation (§7) | Different diffuse-sovereignty layers visibly fail at different legs (platforms fail at *see*; legislatures at *decide*; global commons at *act*) | Makes the triad a diagnostic per-situation, not a universal sequence — possibly the most honest, least general answer |

The riff does **not** pick among these. It records that the only leg with empirical pressure on it (see) leans against strict sequence A and toward B/C/D, and that resolving this is exactly what a triad-level exchange or a §9 path would have to do. The "protocol between the nodes" framing in the originating intent (§1.1) is essentially a vote for C — that the real object is the *interface that does all three*, not three separable machines — which is a hypothesis, not a finding.

## 7. The triad × the layers — functional lens meets #24's structural lens

[Exchange #24](../exchanges/coordination-architecture-reframe-exchange.md) and the [constitutional-ecology riff §3.3](constitutional-ecology-and-coordination-architecture-riff.md) develop a lens of *diffuse-sovereignty layers* — **where** coordination happens (state-electoral, platform-governance, corporate-accountability, financial-infrastructure, AI-systems, transnational-supply-chain, commons; plus the v2.1 additions household/care, religious-institutional, Indigenous/tribal, customary-law, military/security, diaspora, knowledge-production). The triad is a different lens — **what** coordination does (see / decide / act). The natural composition is a **matrix**: every layer has to see, decide, and act, and each cell can fail in its own characteristic way.

The binding-constraint column is what tests T3 (§10.5): if it *varies by layer*, the triad gives information #24's layer-lens alone does not.

## 10. Toward a combined claim set (the Path C precondition)

This is the working claim set the riff is now growing toward — what Path C (§9) needs *before* an exchange opens. It is a **first draft for iteration**, not a finished slate: claims below will be sharpened, and some cut or merged, over successive passes.

### 10.1 The design principle — what a combined exchange must test that separate ones cannot

A combined exchange earns its existence **only if it tests claims a single-leg exchange could not.** If the only claims were per-leg, three separate exchanges (Path A) would be cleaner and lower-risk — each leg tested on its own evidence base, without the overhead of holding three at once. So the set has two tiers:

- **Per-leg claims** — the *components*. The see leg already has these (M1–M5, tested in [#25](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md)); decide and act get first-draft sets here.
- **Triad-level claims** — the *reason to combine* (T-series). These are claims whose truth depends on the *interaction* of the three legs and that are, by construction, untestable leg-by-leg. **The T-series is the load-bearing justification for Path C.** Without it, "combined" is a concatenation and Path A wins.

So the structural answer is **both** — per-leg sets *and* a combined set — but the combined set is **not** a concatenation of the per-leg claims; it is the distinct T-series, with the per-leg claims carried as its components. The see leg's M-claims are **imported at their #25 status, not re-opened** (#25 is at rest); a combined exchange *uses* them — especially M1-weak, the seed of T2 — rather than re-litigating them.

### 10.6 What "solid enough to spawn Path C" looks like

A non-binding readiness bar for turning the set into an exchange:

1. **Each per-leg claim has a falsification condition** (first cut done) **and an evidence base meeting the [Research-Protocol](../process/research-protocol.md) source-count and verification bar for its claim type** — *not* "at least one source." The protocol's T2 floor is **3–15 source digests (or 5–20 inline citations) per gap**, with **≥1 citation from each credible side of any contested claim** ([viewpoint buckets](../../sources/SOURCE_INDEX.md#how-this-index-works)) and a verification pass; that is what makes a claim *established by evidence* rather than asserted (more independent, origin-balanced sources = more robust, not merely more numerous). The one exception is **theorem-grade** content (e.g., D1's single-peakedness / Condorcet core), where a single authoritative source suffices because the discipline is proof, not corroboration. *Not yet met:* D2–D4 and A2–A3 are untested empirical claims needing that multi-source T2 pass (open question Q5).
2. **The triad-level claims are sharp enough to test something emergent** — above all T2, and T3 once the §7 matrix is built out rather than sketched.
3. **The See-import boundary is agreed — and the see evidence meets the same bar.** The combined exchange carries M1–M5 at #25 status and does not re-open them absent new evidence. But import-integrity cuts both ways: if decide/act are held to the multi-source T2 bar (item 1), the imported see claims must meet it too — and [#25 §2.2](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md) explicitly records its see-evidence as sitting *below* the §4.2 verification bar (only an authoring-agent self-check ran, one tier down). So a **see-evidence-strengthening pass is owed** before Path C: run the verification #25 deferred and widen its source base. That pass is scoped to *verification and breadth*, not re-grading; if new evidence materially moves a see claim, re-opening the grading is a steward decision — the #25 *hold* still governs — not an automatic change.
4. **The riskiest claim is named** (open question Q7). T4 — the original candidate — is now built out (§10.8.5) and split: **T4a/T4b survive in weak form and fail in strong form**, so the strong "single unifying object" is no longer the live risk. The riskiest *open* claim shifts to **T2 at systematic-test level** (its §10.8.2 evidence is a curated five, so the selection-bias caveat is unretired) and **T4b's authoring-artifact risk** (the shared signature may be imposed by the riff's own framing). Either failing would show the triad adds little over developing the legs separately (Path A).

Until gates 1–3 are met, the riff stays open and in claim-development mode; spawning Path C is premature.

#### 10.8.1 Research grounding — five cases, origin-balanced

Per [Research Protocol](../process/research-protocol.md) §5, each source carries a link and a verbatim anchor. **Self-verification (§4.3):** the §4.1 checks (source-existence, quote-fidelity, tier) were run by the authoring agent against the fetched page text on June 3, 2026; the load-bearing figures and quotes below were confirmed verbatim, **except** the two vTaiwan stall phrasings ("tiger without teeth," "isegoria evaporated at the threshold"), which are carried from [#25 §2.2](../exchanges/shared-mirror-see-layer-exchange.md)'s prior verbatim confirmation plus 2026 secondary reporting not independently re-fetched in this pass. This grounding sits **below the §4.2 bar** until a verification sub-agent pass or steward spot-check — the same gate #25's grounding is under.

1. **Ireland — Citizens' Assembly → 2018 referendum (see + decide + act wired together → durable change).** A randomly selected mini-public deliberated (decide), its read of public opinion proved accurate (see), and it was coupled to a binding referendum and legislation (act). "It was not a coincidence that the legislative decision following the referendum was the same as that forwarded by the Citizens' Assembly a year earlier"; the assembly "allowed politicians to understand public opinion on the issue before committing to a referendum and a binding legislative decision" ([Participedia](https://participedia.net/case/irish-citizens-assembly-the-eighth-amendment)). *Relevance: the one durable success in the set has all three legs wired.*
2. **Taiwan — vTaiwan / Pol.is (see + decide, act unwired → stalled).** Pol.is surfaced cross-cluster consensus and in the 2015 Uber case the Ministry "pledged to ratify the consensus reached on Pol.is" ([crowd.law](https://congress.crowd.law/case-vtaiwan.html)) — but recommendations were never legally binding; co-creator Jason Hsu called it a "tiger without teeth," and the platform has not driven a major decision since 2018 ([Daily Beast, 2023](https://www.thedailybeast.com/taiwan-tried-to-digitize-democracy-with-vtaiwan-it-was-a-huge-flop/)). One reading: the "isegoria evaporated at the threshold between deliberation and decision" ([Designing Open Democracy, 2026](https://www.designingopendemocracy.com/blog/2026/05/25/taiwans-digital-democracy-experiment-what-it-shows-what-it-doesnt/)). *Relevance: strong see + decide with an unwired act → stall.*
3. **Fact-checking (see-repair works at its own layer; limited downstream).** A four-country meta-analysis found "fact-checks reduced belief in misinformation by 0.59 point on a 5-point scale," and the gains were "robust to the passage of time, with most still evident approximately 2 wk later" ([PNAS, 2020](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2104235118)). But a six-month South Africa trial found "limited effects on news consumption choices or verification behavior," even as discernment improved ([APSR, 2024](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review/article/sustaining-exposure-to-factchecks-misinformation-discernment-media-consumption-and-its-political-implications/C9CC3A0C7BAF7BADBEF0DA6350A875C8)). *Relevance: see-repair succeeds at the see layer but does not by itself produce decide / act change — the M1-weak nuance, and origin-balanced (it cuts both ways).*
4. **Porto Alegre — participatory budgeting (decide + act coupled; decayed when act-commitment withdrew).** PB worked from 1989; "political commitment to the policy declined over time" after 2004, the program "lacked an effective mechanism for incorporating citizens — especially the under-served — in long-term city planning," and it was suspended in 2017 ([World Resources Institute](https://www.wri.org/research/porto-alegre-participatory-budgeting-and-challenge-sustaining-transformative-change)). *Relevance: a working decide + act coupling decays when one leg's (political / act) support is withdrawn — and it hit the act-leg's A1 / A3 scale ceiling.*
5. **Ranked-choice voting (decide-reform alone → limited, often temporary).** The first direct study of RCV and substantive representation found "no significant effect of the reform" and that "RCV produces limited change in the municipalities that have adopted it" ([New America](https://www.newamerica.org/insights/evaluating-the-effects-of-ranked-choice-voting/the-missing-link-rcv-and-substantive-representation-in-local-politics-arjun-vishwanath/)); candidate-pool gains "dissipate after the first election cycle." Origin balance: where RCV is embedded in a fuller system (Australia), the evidence offers "strong support for the moderating, compromise-inducing influence of RCV" ([New America](https://www.newamerica.org/insights/what-we-know-about-ranked-choice-voting/consequences-for-policy-and-politics/)). *Relevance: an isolated decide-layer change moves little durably; the same mechanism wired into a fuller system does more.*

#### 10.8.2 T2 (interdependence) — what the cases show, and what they do not

**The pattern.** The one durable success (Ireland) had see + decide + act wired together. Every stall in the set tracks a missing or withdrawn leg: vTaiwan (act unwired), RCV alone (decide-only), fact-checking (see-only, downstream limited), post-2004 Porto Alegre (act / political commitment withdrawn). This **leans toward T2** — repairing one leg in isolation produced little durable gain; the gains came from coupling.

**What this is not.** Five cases chosen partly *because* they speak to coupling is not a systematic test — selection bias is a live risk (the §2 apophenia trap). I went looking for a clean *disconfirming* case — a single-leg repair that produced durable coordination gain alone — and did not find one in this pass; but absence of a found counter-example is weak evidence, and may reflect the search rather than the world. A real T2 test needs a pre-registered case sample with explicit inclusion rules and a genuine disconfirming search (a [historical-parallel-test](../process/historical-parallel-test-protocol.md)-style design), not a curated five.

**Status:** T2 moves from *first-draft, untested* to **first-pass evidence leans supportive (provisional); selection-bias risk unaddressed; not adversarially tested.**

#### 10.8.3 T1 (decomposition) — does every case localize cleanly?

Each case localizes to one or more legs without residue: vTaiwan (see + decide / act-gap), RCV (decide), fact-checking (see), PB (decide + act), Ireland (all three). No case needed a *fourth* function, and none forced two legs to collapse into one — first-pass support for both **completeness** and **non-redundancy**. Honest caveat: five cases cannot establish completeness (the next failure might not fit), and "localizes cleanly" was an authoring judgment, not an independent coding. **Status:** T1 **first-pass plausible; untested** — needs a larger, independently-coded failure corpus.

#### 10.8.4 T3 (distinctness over #24) — does the binding constraint vary by layer?

The §7.1 matrix's binding-constraint column is *not constant*: platforms bind at the act / decision threshold (vTaiwan), knowledge-production at see-with-an-act-gap (fact-checking), state-electoral at decide↔act coupling (Ireland vs. RCV), commons at act-via-decide (Ostrom). That variation is a per-layer prediction #24's layer-lens alone does not give — **first-pass support for T3.** Caveat: two matrix rows (corporate, AI) are thinly grounded, the financial row only lightly so, the assignments are contestable, and if scrutiny collapses the column to one function the distinctness claim fails. **Status:** T3 **first-pass leans supportive (provisional); contestable; the corporate / AI / financial rows need their own T2 pass.**

#### 10.8.5 T4 (unifying object) — build-out (June 3, 2026)

T4 is the claim §10.6 named **riskiest**, and it is the steward's felt north star — the "protocol between the nodes." Building it out first does two things the project's discipline asks for: it attacks the riskiest claim, and it tests the claim whose failure would most damage the case for a *combined* exchange. The build-out splits T4 into two sub-claims that turned out to have **different** truth values, which is why §10.5 now carries them separately:

- **T4a — leverage locus:** the under-deployed lever in each leg is the *interface between participants* (the rules / protocol / shared structure), not the *nodes* (changing individuals) or a *sovereign* above them.
- **T4b — shared signature:** all three legs share one failure shape — a hidden assumption that makes the function look impossible until the frame shifts.

**Sources for this pass (origin-balanced, theory-grade).** Per [Research Protocol](../process/research-protocol.md) §5 each carries a link and a verbatim anchor; the §4.1 self-checks (source-existence, quote-fidelity, tier) were run by the authoring agent against the fetched page text on June 3, 2026, and every quote below was confirmed verbatim. As with §10.8.1, this grounding sits **below the §4.2 bar** until a verification sub-agent pass or steward spot-check.

*The "leverage is the interface" canon — one anchor per leg, plus a macro anchor:*

- **Decide — mechanism design (2007 Nobel: Hurwicz, Maskin, Myerson).** The field "studies how to construct rules—called mechanisms or institutions—that produce good outcomes according to some predefined metric, even when the designer does not know the players' true preferences or what information they have" ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanism_design); award: [NobelPrize.org 2007](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2007/)). Plain version: you design the *rules of the game* so that self-interested people still reach a good result — the lever is the rules, with the people taken as they are.
- **Act — Ostrom, *Governing the Commons* (1990).** "neither the state nor the market is uniformly successful in enabling individuals to sustain long-term, productive use of natural resource systems. Further, communities of individuals have relied on institutions resembling neither the state nor the market to govern some resource systems with reasonable degrees of success over long periods of time" ([Ostrom 1990, ch. 1](https://www.actu-environnement.com/media/pdf/ostrom_1990.pdf)). The lever is the *institution participants build among themselves* — explicitly **not** a sovereign (the state) and **not** privatization (the market).
- **See — Schelling's focal points + Chwe's common knowledge.** People "concert their mutual expectations on one equilibrium – the focal point – by appealing to their shared knowledge about salient properties of the game" ([Isoni et al. 2019, on Schelling 1960](https://wrap.warwick.ac.uk/id/eprint/113254/14/WRAP-focal-points-payoff-information-in-tacit-bargaining-Isoni-2019.pdf)); and "one crucial function of rituals is to create common knowledge and thereby allow groups to solve coordination problems" ([Chwe, *Rational Ritual*](http://chwe.net/michael/rationalritual_bookproposal.pdf)). The lever is the *shared structure* that lets people coordinate (a landmark everyone latches onto; the state of "everyone knows that everyone knows") — not sharper individual perception.
- **Macro anchor — Acemoglu, Johnson & Robinson (2024 Nobel).** Awarded "for studies of how institutions are formed and affect prosperity" ([NobelPrize.org 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2024/press-release/)) — the leverage-is-institutions thesis at the highest tier.

*The origin-balanced counter — the lever is the nodes (or the sovereign):*

- **Glaeser, La Porta, Lopez-de-Silanes & Shleifer, "Do Institutions Cause Growth?" (2004).** They find "human capital is a more basic source of growth than are the institutions," and that "poor countries get out of poverty through good policies, often pursued by dictators" ([Journal of Economic Growth](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1023/B:JOEG.0000038933.16398.ed)). This is the clean disconfirm: a serious school argues the lever is the *people* (the nodes) — and the "dictators" clause credits the *sovereign*. The institutions-vs-human-capital question is live, not settled (see the [AJR reply](https://economics.mit.edu/sites/default/files/inline-files/Institutions,%20Human%20Capital%20and%20Development.pdf)).

**T4a — what the evidence shows: weak-supported, strong-falsified.** Across all three legs the canonical *solution* literature names an interface/rules lever rather than a node-change or a sovereign-imposition — mechanism design (decide), Ostrom's self-governing institutions (act), focal points and common-knowledge structure (see). That is real, cross-leg support for the **weak** T4a: the interface is a genuine, under-deployed, often-decisive lever the three legs share. But the **strong, exclusive** T4a — "the lever is the interface, *not* the nodes, *not* a sovereign" — fails, on two independent grounds:

1. *Contested at the top tier.* The Glaeser et al. human-capital school holds the lever is the nodes, not the institutions, and the debate remains open even after the 2024 prize. T4a cannot claim "not the nodes" as *established* when Nobel-tier work argues the opposite.
2. *The in-set cases pressure both exclusions.* The sovereign was load-bearing in the one durable success: Ireland worked because the assembly was wired to a **binding** referendum and legislation (a state act), while vTaiwan had the interface but no binding coupling and stalled (§10.8.1) — so "not a sovereign" is too strong. And deliberation (the decide leg's D2) works partly by *changing the participants* — informing them, moving their preferences — a node-change, not a pure interface change, so "not the nodes" is too strong too. Even AJR's "inclusive institutions" fold the state (rule of law, enforced property rights) *into* the institution, blurring the clean interface-vs-sovereign line T4a tries to draw.

So T4a lands exactly where the see leg's M1 did: **strong form falsified, weak form supported.** The defensible claim is that the interface is a *real and chronically-neglected* lever shared across the legs — necessary and under-used — **not** the sole lever, and not one that displaces nodes or sovereign coupling.

**T4b — what the evidence shows: first-pass plausible, authoring-vulnerable, strong form false.** The shared signature is structurally present: each leg in §2 has the form *hidden assumption → looks impossible → reframe → leverage*, and that shape tracks real intellectual history, not only the authoring — Ostrom broke the "commons needs a Leviathan or privatization" dichotomy; mechanism design is "reverse game theory" that builds a game to reach a wanted result; Schelling and Chwe showed that coordination which looked unsolvable is solvable through structure. That is first-pass support for the **weak** T4b (a genuine family resemblance across the legs). But the **strong** T4b fails on two grounds:

1. *Authoring-artifact risk.* The three legs were *selected and framed* in §2 to fit the "hidden assumption" template, so finding the template is partly an artifact of how the riff was built — the §2 apophenia trap, one level up. Retiring this needs an independent corpus, not the riff's own three legs.
2. *The "always dissolves" version is false.* Not every coordination impossibility relaxes under a reframe: Gibbard–Satterthwaite manipulability (§4.3) survives the decide-leg reframe, and some hard or global commons (climate) may genuinely need coercion (§5.3). The honest signature is "a hidden assumption that *sometimes* dissolves," not a law that it always does.

**What the build-out does to Path C.** Attacking the riskiest claim partly succeeded: **T4-strong — a single literal "unifying object" or north-star protocol — does not hold,** which is precisely why §1.1 keeps the north star *felt, not claimed*. What survives — weak T4a (a shared, neglected interface lever) and weak T4b (a family-resemblance signature) — is still genuinely *cross-leg*: neither can be tested one leg at a time, so both still point to a *combined* exchange (Path C) rather than three separate ones (Path A). The combine case therefore does **not** collapse — but it now rests mainly on **T2 (interdependence)** and **T3 (distinctness)**, with T4 contributing weak, contested support rather than a load-bearing "one object" finding. The riskiest open claim shifts accordingly: from "does T4 hold?" (answered: weak yes, strong no) to **T2 at systematic-test level** (retiring the selection-bias caveat of §10.8.2) and **T4b's authoring-artifact risk** (§10.6 gate #4, §11 Q7).
