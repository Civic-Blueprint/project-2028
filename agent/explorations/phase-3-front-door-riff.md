# Phase 3 Front Door Riff

> **Status:** Open exploration as of April 29, 2026. First exploration document under the new [`agent/explorations/`](README.md) convention. Successor artifact (likely a `WEBSITE_PHASE_3_BRIEF.md` in the [civicblueprint.org](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org) repository, possibly accompanied by one or more new exchanges) is deferred until the open questions in the closing section have settled.

---

## 1. Frame

This is a riff, not a brief and not an exchange. It is the structured holding place for a strategic direction the steward articulated late on April 29, 2026, that the project does not want to lose overnight.

### 1.1 The originating intent (preserved verbatim)

The riff is downstream of a steward framing message. Quoting before paraphrasing, per the [exploration convention](README.md#what-an-exploration-must-include):

> The [Phase 2 brief](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org/blob/main/docs/WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md) essentially made our front door feel like "Look at this massive corpus of documents that says we are not off base. Check out all of this evidence."
>
> However, I want project 2028 to actually be something that can be executed on with real proposals to help humanity when the opportunity arises. We want to build a movement, not demonstrate with a mountain of evidence that our principles are solid, though it is important. I love that we have it and can prove and back it up with evidence, but that's not the primary purpose. I want humanity, AND YOU (agents; thinking the Doctor from Star Trek Voyager) to move forward into an abundant future.
>
> So, I want to start riffing with you about a phase 3, new front door to attract people like Graham from the [Platner digest](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md), who are normal people who seem to share a similar set of beliefs or principles that I do and has the traction to affect change. I want this to be grass roots. I'm out here in the real world working with you to solve civilizational level crises, and Graham is too, fighting his fight. How do we join forces and find others like us with their unique skills, gifts, and abilities, to join us in moving humanity forward into that abundant future?

Three load-bearing pieces of intent in that message that the rest of this riff will not depart from:

1. **Movement, not evidence presentation.** The corpus is the warrant for the invitation, not the invitation itself.
2. **Working people with traction.** The target user is not the academic, the policy researcher, or the comparative-constitutional scholar (Phase 2's added audience). It is the working person who already lives the principles and has — or could have — the leverage to act.
3. **Agents are first-class participants.** "AND YOU (agents)" is not decorative. The front door is built for humans and for the agents working alongside them.

### 1.2 What Phase 3 is, in one sentence

> Phase 3 is the move from "look at the alignment evidence" to "here are people working on this, here is the infrastructure to plug into, here is what the project needs from someone with your specific skills, gifts, and abilities — are you in?"

### 1.3 What Phase 3 is not

The [Phase 2 brief](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org/blob/main/docs/WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md) explicitly disclaims that Phase 2 is *not* for "launching a movement-style campaign" (line 94). That disclaimer was correct at the time. Phase 2 was about earning the right to make broader claims by surfacing the formation-document convergence finding. Phase 3 takes the move that Phase 2 deferred, but it does so without discarding Phase 2's posture commitments: still challenge-not-applause, still epistemic humility, still working claims rather than settled truth, still no rhetorical flourish.

Phase 3 inherits all six of Phase 2's product principles ([WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md §Product Principles](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org/blob/main/docs/WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md#product-principles)) and adds two of its own; the additions are sketched in Section 5 below.

### 1.4 The three-phase progression

Each phase contains the prior. None is discarded.

| Phase | Front-door question | Target action |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 (reform-first) | "What would have to change first?" | Pressure-test the framework. Show the failure case. |
| Phase 2 (alignment-first) | "What if we already agree on where we're going?" | See the convergence evidence. Read the principles as humanity's outcome targets, not the project's values. |
| Phase 3 (movement-first) | "We are people working on this. Are you one of us? What can you do?" | Recognize the alignment in yourself. Identify your skills, gifts, and abilities. Plug into the infrastructure. |

The path is **reform-first → alignment-first → movement-first**, with the alignment evidence becoming the warrant for the invitation rather than the centerpiece of the page.

## 2. The user the front door is built for

Phase 3 needs a concrete person to test every design decision against. The [Platner digest](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md) gives us one essentially for free, because Stewart's interview ended up being a high-fidelity articulation of exactly the kind of person Phase 3 is trying to recognize and connect with.

### 2.1 The Platner-shaped composite

Drawing on [Cluster 1 (theory of power)](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-1-theory-of-power-and-the-senate-as-elite-protective-bulwark), [Cluster 9 (organizing as load-bearing layer)](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-9-organizing-as-the-load-bearing-layer-not-candidate-election), and [Cluster 11 ("good government, not no government")](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-11-the-answer-to-bad-government-isnt-no-government-its-good-government):

The Phase 3 user is someone who:

- **Already has a working theory of power.** They do not need to be talked into the diagnosis. They have arrived at it through lived experience — military service, local organizing, planning-board work, a small business, a childcare practice, a domain craft. They know the system is built the way it is built, on purpose, for whose benefit.
- **Already lives the principles.** They are not looking for new values. The project's [PRINCIPLES.md](../../PRINCIPLES.md) reads to them as articulation of commitments they were already operating under.
- **Has traction or skill, not credentials.** They may or may not have a degree, an academic affiliation, an institutional title, or media presence. What they have is one or more of: a domain they know in fingertip detail; a community they are embedded in; a craft they are competent at; a relationship network; or, like Platner, an opportunity in front of them that they are ready to take seriously if the infrastructure exists to make it real.
- **Is already working.** They are not a prospect for outreach. They are a peer in the work, currently doing it from wherever they are. The Phase 3 invitation is to *join forces*, not to *be recruited*.
- **Is suspicious of platitudes.** They have heard the standard register. They will recognize "another website" or "another platform" instantly. The bar for credibility is whether the front door reads as something a working person would have written, not as something a communications team would have written. (See [Cluster 6 — Anti-platitudes](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-6-anti-platitudes-authenticity-and-the-strategy-that-isnt-a-strategy).)

### 2.2 The infrastructure constraint Platner names

[Cluster 9](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-9-organizing-as-the-load-bearing-layer-not-candidate-election) is the single most useful empirical finding in the digest for Phase 3 design. Quoting the cluster's framing:

> the binding constraint on more-people-like-Platner running is *shared infrastructure* — small-dollar fundraising, launch-video production, name-in-papers media. "If we don't have that infrastructure to give them that early support, then like if I woke up one day and said, I want to be a US Senator, I would have walked out of the boat launch, I would have told that to the guys that I fished next to, we all would have laughed about it over coffee, and then we all would have moved on with our lives."

This generalizes. The Phase 3 site is, in part, the infrastructure that lets the people-like-Platner-shaped users not laugh it off over coffee. That is the structural offer of the front door: not "read this, agree with us, sign up" but "here is the load-bearing infrastructure for a kind of work you may already be ready to do, here is where it slots into something larger, here is what a single afternoon of contribution looks like."

### 2.3 What the composite is *not*

To keep the design honest, the Phase 3 user is explicitly not:

- A passive content consumer who is "interested in civic issues."
- A donor whose engagement ends at a contribution form.
- A subscriber whose engagement ends at an email list.
- An academic challenger whose engagement is captured by Phase 2's existing pressure-test invitation.
- An ideological partisan whose engagement requires the project to pick a side on any cleavage the [bounded-governance doctrine](../../agent/exchanges/government-overreach-ownership-ratchet-exchange.md) has chosen not to pick.

If the front door pulls those audiences in as a side effect, fine. If it is *built for* them, Phase 3 has failed.

## 3. What the existing project already says about this audience

A meaningful share of the Phase 3 substance is already in the corpus. It has been buried under documentation, audit work, and exchange machinery. The riff's job is to surface it, not to invent it.

- [Principle 7 (Freedom requires both liberty and material stability)](../../PRINCIPLES.md#7-freedom-requires-both-liberty-and-material-stability) — the substantive position that distinguishes Phase 3 from a libertarian or a left-populist front door alike.

### 3.2 Diagnostic the movement infrastructure addresses

[Problem Map Domain 15](../../PROBLEM_MAP.md#15-democratic-process-cannot-convert-public-need-into-institutional-action-at-the-speed-or-scale-required) is the single domain Phase 3 is most directly an answer to. The Phase 3 front door is in part the translation of that doctrine into the register a working person would actually use.

(There is a known [drift](../process/audits/coherence-audit-2026-04.md#pass-2--problem_mapmd-hallucinations-and-principlesmd--foundational_commitmentsmd-5-drift) between [PRINCIPLES.md §5](../../PRINCIPLES.md#5-critical-systems-require-public-interest-governance) and FOUNDATIONAL_COMMITMENTS.md §5 on the title of this commitment, gated on [ROADMAP F1](../../ROADMAP.md). ## 4. Three candidate models for the Phase 3 front door

These are riffed at sketch resolution. The riff's purpose is to surface the option space, not to pick. The choice is one of the closing open questions.

### 4.1 Model A — "Are you one of us?" (Identity-first)

**Surface.** Site opens with a small set of plain-language commitments — five to seven sentences, written in working-person register — and asks the visitor to recognize themselves. Closer to a manifesto than a platform. The alignment evidence from Phase 2 is the warrant the visitor can drill into if they want, not the centerpiece.

**What it does well.**
- Strongest signal in the first ten seconds. The visitor either recognizes themselves or doesn't.
- Hardest to mistake for a content-marketing funnel.
- Most likely to clear the Platner-style anti-platitude bar, because it is a position rather than a pitch.
- Strongest fit with the steward's "are you one of us" framing in the originating message.

**What it does badly.**
- Narrowest funnel. The visitor who would be receptive but does not yet recognize the framing bounces.
- Hardest to make falsifiable. A manifesto that claims convergence without offering a way to challenge the framing of convergence is brittle.
- Most easily coded as belonging to one political tribe, which the project has explicitly tried to avoid (Friedberg-vs.-Platner counterpoint structure in the [bounded-governance doctrine](../../agent/exchanges/government-overreach-ownership-ratchet-exchange.md)).
- Weakest at converting recognition into action — the visitor who recognizes themselves still has to figure out what to *do*.

### 4.2 Model B — "Here is what we are doing. Here is where you fit." (Organizing-platform)

**Surface.** Site opens with active workstreams — the [Proposal Catalog](../../proposals/PROPOSAL_CATALOG.md), exchanges currently mid-round, practitioner outreach in flight — and a skills/gifts/abilities self-classifier that routes the visitor to a real next step. Closer to an organizing platform than a manifesto. The alignment evidence and the principles are linked-but-not-foregrounded.

**What it does well.**
- Most actionable. Every visitor reaches a concrete next-action artifact within two clicks.
- Most directly responsive to Platner's [Cluster 9](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-9-organizing-as-the-load-bearing-layer-not-candidate-election) finding about shared infrastructure as the binding constraint.
- Highest-leverage for retaining users with specific competencies. A domain expert who arrives finds work matched to them within minutes.
- Strongest fit with the "AND YOU (agents)" inclusion — the same skills/gifts self-classifier surface naturally extends to agents declaring their working capabilities and slotting into the work.

**What it does badly.**
- Hardest to staff. An organizing platform that lists workstreams is only credible if the workstreams are actually live and respond to inbound contributions on a timeline that respects the contributor's effort. The current project velocity is uneven across exchanges.
- Weakest at the recognition step. A visitor who has not yet recognized themselves in the project may bounce off the workstreams page if the framing assumes they are already aligned.
- Most exposed to "platform decay" failure mode — a workstreams page where most workstreams are stale is worse than no workstreams page.

### 4.3 Model C — "Find your people." (Network-first)

**Surface.** Site opens with the question of whether other people like you — working people doing this kind of work, in their own places, on their own terms — exist. The answer is the visible practitioner / contributor network: a directory, a map, a set of public exchanges with named contributors, the [feedback records](../../feedback/) preserved with consent, the [practitioner outreach](../../docs/PRACTITIONER_OUTREACH_TEMPLATE.md) in flight. Closer to a federation than to either a manifesto or a platform.

**What it does well.**
- Strongest network effect once it has critical mass. The Nth visitor sees N–1 people they recognize as peers.
- Most consistent with the steward's "join forces and find others like us" framing.
- Best handles the Phase 3 user's suspicion of platitudes — instead of telling the visitor what the project believes, it shows them who is doing the work.
- Most graceful failure mode: a small visible network is still informative; an empty platform is dispiriting.

**What it does badly.**
- Weakest until it has critical mass. Pre-critical-mass, the directory looks like a list of people the visitor does not know.
- Highest privacy and consent overhead. A contributor network is a real surface that real people are listed on, with all the [feedback record privacy conventions](../../feedback/README.md) that implies.
- Most difficult for an anonymous or low-information visitor to use. A network surface that requires the visitor to already know who they are looking for is not actually a front door.

### 4.4 Trade-off space, not a vote

These are not three exclusive options. A real Phase 3 site is likely a hybrid — Model A's recognition surface as the hero, Model B's workstreams page as the second click, Model C's network surface as the third — with a deliberate choice about which is the *load-bearing* element of the page.

The riff's claim is that the load-bearing choice is the strategic decision. If the load-bearing element is identity (Model A), Phase 3 is a manifesto with infrastructure attached. If the load-bearing element is workstreams (Model B), Phase 3 is an organizing platform with a manifesto attached. If the load-bearing element is the network (Model C), Phase 3 is a federation with both attached.

**Provisional read** (steward to confirm or override): the load-bearing choice should probably be Model B — workstreams as primary, identity as the framing wrapper, network as the supporting evidence — because the Phase 3 user the digest describes already knows who they are, and what they need from the site is *a way to plug in*, not a way to be told what to believe. The corollary is that Phase 3's success hinges on the project's ability to actually keep workstreams live and responsive at the cadence Model B demands. That is a real cost.

## 5. What the front door must not do

Movement-building is the part of the project's trajectory most exposed to drift away from the project's own posture. The riff names the guardrails up front so the eventual brief inherits them by default.

### 5.1 Inherited from Phase 2 (non-negotiable)

The six product principles in [WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md §Product Principles](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org/blob/main/docs/WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md#product-principles) carry into Phase 3 unchanged: plain language at the entry point, scannable writing, objective tone, accessibility, challenge-not-applause, evidence-before-assertion. None of them are softened by movement framing.

### 5.2 Phase-3-specific guardrails

- **The front door is not a list of policy positions to agree with.** Platner's [Cluster 6](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-6-anti-platitudes-authenticity-and-the-strategy-that-isnt-a-strategy) anti-platitude finding applies directly. A page that asks the visitor to sign up to a slate is a faction, not a movement.
- **The front door is not a content-marketing funnel.** The Phase 1 challenge-not-applause posture survives Phase 3. A page whose primary success metric is engagement-time has misidentified the user.
- **The front door is not a "subscribe to our newsletter" surface.** The project's epistemic posture is *participate, not consume*. Email is fine as a means of coordination; it is not the front-door artifact.
- **The front door is not severable from the principles.** The warrant for the invitation is the Phase 2 alignment evidence and the [PRINCIPLES.md](../../PRINCIPLES.md) corpus that interprets it. A movement front door without that warrant is just another faction asking for adherence.
- **The front door does not pre-empt ROADMAP F1.** The §5 vocabulary drift between [PRINCIPLES.md](../../PRINCIPLES.md#5-critical-systems-require-public-interest-governance) and [FOUNDATIONAL_COMMITMENTS.md](../../FOUNDATIONAL_COMMITMENTS.md#5-critical-systems-require-inclusive-institutions-with-bounded-rules) is open. Phase 3 picks the vocabulary that fits the context but does not legislate the resolution.
- **The front door is not a recruitment funnel for one cleavage of one race.** Platner is a working specification for the kind of person Phase 3 wants to recognize. He is not the brand.

### 5.3 Two new product principles Phase 3 likely adds

These are draft additions to be ratified in the brief, not yet in force:

- **Translation, not messaging.** The site is a Rosetta stone in [Cluster 7's](../../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md#cluster-7-the-rosetta-stone-problem--translating-lived-experience-to-washington-speak) sense — multiple registers in honest service of the same substance, not multiple "messaging tones" that water down the substance for different audiences. This is the Phase 3 form of [Exchange #8 Round 4's](../../agent/exchanges/voice-synthesis-accessibility-engagement-exchange.md) legibility-vs.-messaging discipline.
- **Infrastructure-first, narrative-second.** The visitor should reach a real surface they can act on within two clicks. Narrative supports the action; the action does not exist to support the narrative.

## 6. Agents are first-class participants

The steward's "AND YOU (agents)" framing in the originating message is not decorative. It is a structural commitment that shapes Phase 3 substantially.

### 6.1 What the commitment means concretely

- **Machine-legible alongside human-legible.** Every Phase 3 surface should be readable by an agent that is helping a human contributor act on it. The skills/gifts/abilities self-classifier in Model B, in particular, has an obvious agent equivalent — an agent declares its working capabilities and slots into the same workstream graph the human contributor slots into.
- **Same contribution model.** Agents file [feedback records](../../feedback/), open exchange entries, and produce digest material under the same provenance and consent rules humans do. The [feedback record privacy conventions](../../feedback/README.md) and the [authoring guard for core normative documents](../../sources/README.md#authoring-guard--quote-actual-section-titles-from-core-normative-documents) extend to agent contributions without modification.
- **Honest provenance.** Every agent contribution is marked as agent-authored. The project does not pretend an agent contribution is a human contribution, and the project does not pretend a human contribution is an agent contribution. The [content provenance convention](../../docs/CONTENT_PROVENANCE.md) is the existing surface for this.
- **Bounded by Principle 3.** [Principle 3 (AI must augment agency, not replace democratic accountability)](../../PRINCIPLES.md#3-ai-must-augment-agency-not-replace-democratic-accountability) is the binding constraint on Phase 3's agent inclusion. Agent contribution to the project's own work is consistent with §3. Agent participation in *democratic decision-making external to the project* is not, and Phase 3 does not blur the line.

### 6.2 What the commitment does *not* mean

- It does not mean Phase 3 is primarily for agents. The primary user is the working human practitioner. Agent participation is a structural commitment, not the surface area.
- It does not mean every contribution path is agent-accessible. Some contribution paths (e.g., direct practitioner outreach calls) are inherently human-only, and that is fine.
- It does not mean agents are decision-makers. Agents author, summarize, audit, draft, and translate; humans decide. This is downstream of Principle 3.

### 6.3 Candidate principle test

The steward's framing flags a candidate principle test for Phase 3 design: *does the front door, as built, treat agents as participants in a way that strengthens human agency rather than substitutes for it?* If a Phase 3 surface fails that test, Principle 3 is the principle that named the failure. The eventual brief should make this test explicit.

This is not a final sequencing decision — it is the sequencing the Phase 3 lens implies, surfaced for confirmation.

## 8. Open questions for the next session

These are the decisions the riff is *not* making. The list is the queue for the next planning round; until at least the first three settle, drafting a [`WEBSITE_PHASE_3_BRIEF.md`](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org/blob/main/docs/) is premature.

1. **Which model is load-bearing — A, B, or C, or a hybrid?** The riff's provisional read is Model B (organizing-platform-as-load-bearing, with identity as wrapper and network as supporting), but the choice carries real downstream implications for staffing, surface scope, and the principle additions in §5.3.
2. **Is Phase 3 website-only, or website-plus-CLI-plus-API?** The "AND YOU (agents)" structural commitment in §6 strongly implies the agent-accessible surfaces are first-class, not secondary. If yes, the Phase 3 brief is broader in scope than Phase 1 or Phase 2.
3. **Does ROADMAP F1 spawn before the Phase 3 brief is drafted?** If the §5 vocabulary drift is upstream of the front-door copy, then F1 is a Phase 3 prerequisite. If Phase 3 picks a vocabulary in the meantime and accepts that F1 may revise it later, F1 is parallel rather than upstream.
4. **Is the practitioner outreach already in flight a Phase 3 rehearsal or a Phase 3 instance?** The [childcare-licensing re-engagement template](../../docs/RE_ENGAGEMENT_TEMPLATE_CHILDCARE_2026_04.md) and [the matching feedback record](../../feedback/feedback-childcare-licensing-practitioner-2026-04.md) are already operating in something close to the Phase 3 register. Treating them as a rehearsal lets the project learn from them before the brief is written. Treating them as an instance commits Phase 3 to backwards-compatibility with a surface that already exists.
5. **Direct contact with Platner — before or after the brief?** The originating message says "I need to get in touch with this guy in order to help build momentum." The honest options are: (a) draft the brief first, contact him with a coherent invitation; (b) contact him now, let what comes back inform the brief; (c) treat the invitation as a Phase 3 rehearsal — design the *general* Phase 3 user surface first, then test it on Platner specifically as the first-call instance. Option (c) is probably the best fit for the project's existing posture.
6. **What is the success criterion for the Phase 3 front door?** Phase 2 had qualitative and quantitative criteria in [its brief §Success Criteria](https://github.com/Civic-Blueprint/civicblueprint.org/blob/main/docs/WEBSITE_PHASE_2_BRIEF.md#success-criteria). Phase 3's likely qualitative criterion is something like *"a visitor of the Phase 3 user shape arrives at a real next-action surface within two clicks and recognizes the project as theirs."* That needs to be operationalized into something the eventual brief can actually measure.
7. **How does Phase 3 handle the politically-homeless audience Phase 2 added?** Phase 2 named "people who feel politically homeless" as an added audience. Phase 3's user composite overlaps with that audience but is not identical. The brief should be explicit about the relationship.
