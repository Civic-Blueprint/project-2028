# Feedback Record — Grassroots Movement Contact, June 2026

> **Status (June 2026):** Preserved record. This file captures a real, unprompted interaction in which a person in the steward's personal network (a) independently articulated a diagnosis that closely tracks the project's bounded-governance / capture framing, and (b) offered to produce shareable media and point traffic to the project's website. No identifying information is included. The interaction feeds the [Phase 3 Front Door Riff](../agent/explorations/phase-3-front-door-riff.md) (see §4.7), where the analysis lives.
>
> **Why this record exists:** The contact is a high-fidelity, real-world instance of the Phase 3 "user the front door is built for" — a working person who already has a theory of power and wants to *act*. The project preserves the exact words because future Phase 3 design decisions (and a possible re-engagement) should reason from what the person actually said, not from a paraphrase that drifts. This is also the project's **first inbound-traffic-partner signal**: someone offering to route an audience *to* the front door, which is a distinct case from the practitioner-review records already on file. A [same-day follow-up](#image-artifact-and-nonviolence-framing-same-day-follow-up-june-19-2026) — the referenced image plus an explicit Gandhian nonviolence framing — adds a **posture / legitimacy-of-mobilization** dimension: the imagery the ally is drawn to is insurrectionary even though his stated intent is nonviolent, which is the load-bearing tension for whether and how the project can accept the channel.

## Interaction context

The contact opened by telling the steward about a back-and-forth they had been having that day with a third party — described by the contact as a friend who is far to the political right. The contact's self-description is that they are "neither right nor left." They then pasted, for the steward to read, the most recent message they had sent that friend: a structured argument that the rigging of the system is bipartisan ("both teams running the same play for the same owners"), built around regulatory capture and a list of specific historical examples attributed to both parties.

The steward agreed and reframed the agreement in the project's own terms (it is capture over time, not left-vs-right). The contact then escalated from analysis to a proposal: they want to produce shareable media (an animation with scrolling explainer text) about protecting an intergenerational standard of living, make messages designed to go viral, and — the part most relevant to the project — point some of that audience back to the steward's website "to make people aware" and have them "read up on the topic."

The third party (the "friend") is referenced but not identified; no identifying details about either person are recorded. The image the contact references ("that image I shared with you before") was subsequently forwarded by the steward, along with the contact's commentary on it and a note that the two had separately discussed the Netflix series *Arcane* in the same connection. The image is described — but **not** stored — in the [same-day follow-up section](#image-artifact-and-nonviolence-framing-same-day-follow-up-june-19-2026) below; the binary is kept out of version control for provenance reasons explained there.

1. **A second real-world instance of the Phase 3 user shape.** The [Platner digest](../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-platner-digest.md) gave the project a *media-derived* specification of the Phase 3 user. A warm ally proposing exactly that mode, pointed at the front door, is the cleanest possible test of whether the project can *receive* messaging-first traffic without *becoming* a messaging funnel.

### Decoded carefully

- **"highlights of how much debt... jobs the average American now holds... average price of homes"** — the data layer he wants to surface. These map onto the project's material-stability spine ([Principle 7](../PRINCIPLES.md#7-freedom-requires-both-liberty-and-material-stability), [Principle 2](../PRINCIPLES.md#2-essential-needs-should-not-be-held-hostage-to-avoidable-scarcity)) and the [Problem Map](../PROBLEM_MAP.md). The same downstream-use caution applies: any figure the project itself repeats must clear [Research-Protocol](../agent/process/research-protocol.md) verification.
- **"the Gandhi thing... I don't want to inspire violence but I do want to inspire passive strong resistance"** — an explicit, unprompted commitment to nonviolent resistance. On *stated intent*, the contact draws exactly the line the project's [Principle 17 (collective power within principled constraints)](../PRINCIPLES.md#17-collective-power-must-be-exercised-within-principled-constraints) draws.
- **"I think we're past the point now where I feel like I have to do something"** — a threshold-crossed urgency. This is the motivational engine behind the offer, and also the register most exposed to drift toward the escalation he disclaims.
- **The aesthetic-vs-intent gap.** The image he is drawn to speaks in the *opposite* register from his stated intent: "IF WE BURN YOU BURN WITH US!" is burn-it-down, mutually-assured-destruction imagery from a story about violent revolt; his words are Gandhian. The distance between the *aesthetic* (insurrectionary) and the *intent* (nonviolent) is the single most important thing this follow-up surfaces, because the project's website would be the **destination** the imagery points at.

### Why this is a posture guardrail, not only a tension

The first exchange surfaced a viral-vs-legible tension. This follow-up adds a sharper one: an inbound channel whose *imagery* is insurrectionary feeding a project whose [bounded-governance doctrine](../agent/exchanges/government-overreach-ownership-ratchet-exchange.md) and [Principle 17](../PRINCIPLES.md#17-collective-power-must-be-exercised-within-principled-constraints) explicitly reject mobilization outside principled constraints. The contact's stated nonviolence is genuine and aligned; the aesthetic he reaches for is not. The front door cannot be the endpoint of a "burn it down" funnel without violating its own doctrine — *regardless of the upstream author's sincere intent*. This is the [Exchange #21 F3 "legitimacy of mobilization"](../agent/exchanges/government-overreach-ownership-ratchet-exchange.md) question made concrete.

### What this follow-up does *not* prove

- It does not prove the contact would carry the nonviolent line into execution; stated intent is not a track record.
- It does not establish the image's provenance, authorship, or license.
- It does not decide whether the project should accept, decline, or reshape the collaboration. That is a steward decision the riff frames, not one this record makes.
