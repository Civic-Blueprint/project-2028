# A Public Agenda for AI Goods — Walking by Example with Legible Civic Instruments

> **Provenance:** `collaborative` — human-directed AI drafting, steward synthesis and approval pending. See [Content Provenance Standard](../docs/CONTENT_PROVENANCE.md).

> **Status and intended use.** This is a **working-claim** position memo, drafted as the **Thread D** deliverable from [The Collapse of American Politics riff §5](../agent/explorations/the-collapse-of-american-politics-attention-abundance-and-ai-goods-riff.md#5-thread-d--walk-by-example-fight-unregulated-algorithms-with-whats-already-built-tangible-ai-safety) and its [§10 routing disposition](../agent/explorations/the-collapse-of-american-politics-attention-abundance-and-ai-goods-riff.md#10-steward-dispositions-and-routing-june-26-2026). It is the **construction-side companion** to the method-side [Decorrelation memo](decorrelation-independent-ai-evaluation-memo.md): that memo argues *how to evaluate* (Door 1); this one argues *what to build* (the positive AI-goods agenda) and *how a small project can honestly contribute* (by example, as an existence proof). Like its companion, it is **not yet cleared for external use** — per the project's own discipline, a memo that preaches a discipline must first pass it (§8). Nothing here is adopted by being written down. The source prompt (Ezra Klein) is a [**near-ally, not an authority**](../sources/source-modern-wisdom-1114-klein-digest.md#interpretive-notes); his framing is used as a prompt, not a warrant.

## 1. The claim in one paragraph

The public AI-safety conversation is heavily weighted toward **what to prevent** and thin on **what to build** — the public goods a well-aimed AI agenda could furnish. Two moves close that gap. First, **regulate the system you have**: you cannot govern a problem whose shape you do not know, so safety needs a *present-tense, tangible object* to act on, not only a hypothetical future. Second, **a theory of AI goods**: ask what public goods to build, recognizing that AI solves problems that are (a) funded and (b) *legible* — structured so a machine can learn them (the AlphaFold shape). For a small, early-stage project the honest contribution is **not** to out-regulate the feed or claim frontier-risk depth; it is to **build legible civic instruments** — a decorrelated evaluator, a calibration ledger, a see-layer census — that work as a **present-tense existence proof that a different objective function is possible.** The deliverable that makes this disciplined rather than self-congratulatory is a **four-question test** that any such instrument must pass to count as walking by example *without becoming the machine it diagnoses* (§6).

## 2. The prompt, separated from the source

The memo is downstream of [Modern Wisdom #1114 (Klein)](../sources/source-modern-wisdom-1114-klein-digest.md), used as a prompt. Two of its moves are load-bearing here; both are stated as Klein's framing, not as adopted findings.

- **Regulate the system you have, not the hypothetical** ([digest Cluster 5](../sources/source-modern-wisdom-1114-klein-digest.md#cluster-5-ai-safety--regulate-the-system-you-have-not-the-hypothetical)). "You cannot solve a problem whose shape you do not know… the political system needs to get in the game on the system that exists right now." This is a **capacity-building** argument: you earn the ability to govern the tail by governing the present. It is in productive tension with the existential-risk camp the corpus also carries, and the memo does **not** adopt it as a verdict on tail risk — it borrows only the narrower point that *safety benefits from a tangible, present-tense object to act on.* (Balance per the [near-ally note](../sources/source-modern-wisdom-1114-klein-digest.md#interpretive-notes): pair with the [AI existential-risk](../sources/source-ai-existential-risk-digest.md) reading whenever cited.)
- **A theory of AI goods** ([digest Cluster 4](../sources/source-modern-wisdom-1114-klein-digest.md#cluster-4-a-theory-of-ai-goods--power-concentration-legibility-and-the-public-agenda-for-ai)). "We are having so many conversations about what we don't want from AI. What do we want from it? What are the public goods?" — operationalized via prizes / advance-market-commitments and **gated on data legibility** (AlphaFold worked because the protein-structure data was already the cleanest scientific database in existence; for most problems "you will have to *create* the data"). This is the genuinely **not-yet-in-corpus** harvest: the corpus is rich on AI *harms and governance* and thin on a *positive public agenda for AI.*

The project's standing position already converges with the first move from the institutional side — see the [Proof-of-Usefulness memo, Case 2 (AI governance)](proof-of-usefulness-memo-01.md#case-2-ai-governance), which frames the governance gap as an institutional-capacity problem and treats AI as dual-character (risk **and** tool). This memo extends that into a sharper, narrower claim about the project's *own built instruments.*

## 3. The reframe: from defense to construction

The instinct behind Thread D is "fight unregulated algorithms with what we've already built." The honest form of that is **not** "we will out-regulate the feed" — a small project cannot, and claiming it would be the overreach the corpus criticizes. The honest form is **construction**:

> The contribution is to build the *legible civic instruments* that demonstrate a different objective function is possible — and let the working artifact, not an argument, be the evidence.

That is exactly the "tangible, present-tense object" Klein says AI safety lacks, and it is the one thing a small project can actually furnish: **not scale, but an existence proof of a different design.** An engagement-optimized feed is an instrument with an objective function (attention capture). The claim is not that the project's instruments out-compete it at scale; it is that they **exist** and optimize for something else (legibility, decorrelated judgment, plural self-definition), which refutes the premise that the feed's objective function is the only one on offer. An existence proof is a modest but real epistemic object: it does not show the alternative *wins*; it shows it is *possible*, which is precisely what a "we can't even imagine an alternative" discourse is missing.

This is a different "walk by example" than [Proof-of-Usefulness memo 01](proof-of-usefulness-memo-01.md). That memo demonstrates the *framework's analytical usefulness* (housing vs. AI, institutional capacity as the upstream bet). This memo demonstrates the *project's built method-instruments themselves* as the object. The two are complementary: one shows the analysis travels; this one shows the tools exist.

## 5. The legibility precondition is two-edged

Klein's gate — AI solves problems that are *legible* to the system — is the constructive face of a property the corpus worries about elsewhere. **Legibility enables AI goods *and* enables surveillance/control.** The same database integration that lets an IRS model "do your taxes with you" is, in a different hand, the [Muskism digest's](../sources/source-weekly-show-stewart-slobodian-muskism-digest.md) DOGE database integration "for control, not efficiency." So a public-agenda-for-AI-goods program cannot treat "make the problem legible" as an unalloyed good:

- **Legibility for goods** must be paired with [Principle 4](../PRINCIPLES.md#4-power-must-remain-accountable-legible-and-reversible) governance of the resulting data surface (who can see it, challenge it, reverse it) — otherwise the instrument that was built to furnish a good becomes a control surface.
- This is why the see-layer instruments in §4 are scoped **see-not-decide** and carry privacy postures by construction: the project's own instruments must model the discipline they prescribe, or they fail their own test (§6).

This is the memo's main *sharpening* of the Klein prompt: a theory of AI goods is incomplete without a theory of which legibility is safe to create, governed by P4.

## 6. The operational test — "walk by example" without "becoming a better machine"

A project that is itself heavily AI-assisted and claims to "fight unregulated algorithms" must carry the standing reflexive firewall: **it must not become the machine it diagnoses.** A warning is not enough; this is the memo's concrete deliverable — a four-question check any tangible artifact must pass *before* the project ships it as "walking by example." (Drafted at [Collapse riff §10.3](../agent/explorations/the-collapse-of-american-politics-attention-abundance-and-ai-goods-riff.md#103-oq6--the-walk-by-example-without-becoming-a-better-machine-test); recorded here as the spec.)

1. **Augment-or-replace.** Does it return a *better-reasoned human decision*, or a *faster output that bypasses human reasoning*? Fails if the human's role shrinks to rubber-stamping. ([Principle 3](../PRINCIPLES.md#3-ai-must-augment-agency-not-replace-democratic-accountability).)
2. **Gym-or-sermon.** Does it make the user *do the reasoning*, or *deliver a conclusion* more efficiently? A more-efficient sermon fails; the point is to load the civic muscle, not pre-chew the answer.
3. **Legible-not-controlling.** Is the output *appealable and inspectable* by the person it affects, or an unappealable "data point"? ([Principle 4](../PRINCIPLES.md#4-power-must-remain-accountable-legible-and-reversible); the §5 two-edged-legibility constraint.)
4. **Decorrelation-preserving.** Does it *widen* the range of independent judgment, or *narrow* it toward a single objective function (the thing it diagnoses)? Fails if it becomes one more correlated feed. ([Verifier memo](agent-automation-and-the-verifier-memo.md): *convergence can be shared bias, not truth.*)

An instrument that passes all four is a candidate existence proof; one that fails any is the project quietly becoming a better machine. The test is **drafted, not validated** — it is itself a working claim that should be challenged in the review at §8.

## 7. Honest limits

- **Existence proof ≠ proof of scale or of victory.** The strongest form of this memo's claim is that a different objective function is *possible*, demonstrated by working artifacts. It does **not** show the alternative out-performs an engagement-optimized feed at population scale, and it must not be read that way.
- **n = 1, agent-scale, mostly documents.** The instruments are proofs of mechanism (one harness run; protocols and sketches), not a deployed track record. "Tools" is generous; several are conventions, not running software.
- **Not domain depth.** The project cannot evaluate bio/cyber uplift or loss-of-control behavior, and claims no frontier-risk authority. The contribution is at the **instrument/method** layer only.
- **Near-ally convergence is not proof.** That the project's instruments fit Klein's "AI goods" prompt so neatly is a convergence the project's own discipline says not to trust; per the [near-ally rule](../agent/explorations/the-collapse-of-american-politics-attention-abundance-and-ai-goods-riff.md#104-oq7--using-a-near-ally-without-partisan-capture), Klein is never the sole warrant, and the capacity-building framing is balanced against the tail-risk camp.
- **Reflexive self-implication.** A heavily AI-assisted memo arguing for human-augmenting instruments is exactly the artifact §6 polices; it must pass its own four-question test, and the recommendation to "build legible instruments" can itself become a sermon if it is not held to a real next-action surface.
- **Single lineage.** This memo was drafted in one (Claude) lineage and is therefore maximally correlated with the riff it derives from. `[single-lineage — uncross-checked]`

## 8. Self-application (the discipline this memo must pass)

A memo arguing that the project should "walk by example" with instruments that augment rather than replace human reasoning cannot credibly be released without (a) surviving decorrelated review itself and (b) passing its own §6 test. Before any external delivery or publication this memo routes to the [Cross-Lineage Review Harness](../agent/process/cross-lineage-review-harness-protocol.md): blind, independent-lineage review, divergence preserved, human go/no-go. The §6 four-question test should be run *against the memo's own recommended artifacts* in that pass. If it cannot survive the discipline it preaches, it does not go out. This is also the cleanest demonstration of the claim — the method applied to the argument for the method (the same posture as the [Decorrelation memo §6](decorrelation-independent-ai-evaluation-memo.md)).
