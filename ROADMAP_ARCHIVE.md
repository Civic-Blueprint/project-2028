## Completed research sweeps and exchange rounds

### Exchange #21 — Government Overreach / Ownership / Ratchet

**Completed phases (April 2026):**

- **Round 1 — constructive opening.** Reframed the ratchet as a broader capture dynamic rather than a state-only phenomenon; argued ownership likely persists under abundance with differentiated moral weight; named bounded-governance design as the unresolved crux.
- **First research sweep.** 43 curated source digests across eight sub-debates, with balanced viewpoint coverage (pro-market/libertarian; social-democratic/progressive; heterodox/institutional).
- **Round 2 — sub-debate stress test.** Produced the five-ratchet typology (scope / commitment / cascade / drift / concentration); four-category ownership taxonomy; five-mechanism democratic-capture inventory (Caplan / Gilens-Page / Hacker-Pierson / Bartels / Kuran-Sunstein); institutional-inclusiveness reframing of Principle 5 (Acemoglu-Robinson-Rodrik rather than state-vs-market); sector-specific Perry-chart decomposition (concede housing, reform-concentration-not-retreat healthcare, mixed education); nine-element bounded-governance design package grounded in Swiss debt brake, OECD/IMF fiscal rules, Buchanan-Tullock, and Ostrom polycentricity.
- **Second research sweep.** 7 additional digests closing all five Round 2 data gaps: Argentina/Milei; Canada/Sweden/NZ fiscal-consolidation cases; AI catastrophic-risk literature (Bengio, Russell, Bostrom, IDAIS); AI governance practice (EU AI Act, California SB 1047/SB 53, U.S. EO sequence, NIST AI RMF); cooperative and platform-cooperative ownership (Mondragón); sovereign wealth funds (Norway GPFG, Alaska Permanent Fund); Swiss direct democracy.
- **Round 2 addendum.** Recorded what the new evidence shifted: ownership taxonomy expands to five categories (adds collective-dividend); design package is empirically grounded in five working national instances; democratic contraction is empirically possible but requires a fiscal-crisis enabling condition; direct democracy is a useful entrenchment mechanism but not a universal policy instrument; distributional incidence matters decisively for political sustainability.
- **Round 3 — synthesis.** Produced five commissioned deliverables: revised Principle 5 draft (v1); six-category ownership taxonomy (added collective-dividend); nine-element bounded-governance doctrine; frontier-AI worked example; distributional-incidence addendum.
- **Round 4 — adversarial review.** Applied the [Adversarial Review Protocol](agent/process/adversarial-review-protocol.md) to Round 3's deliverables under Option B (assertions to be tested) plus Option C (domain lenses: public-administration scholar, community organizer, Global South constitutional scholar, heterodox economist, AI-safety-skeptical technologist, Ostrom-tradition commons scholar, post-liberal political theorist). Registered ten clear adversarial wins, two partial wins, two draws, one defended position.
- **Round 5 — constructive integration.** Produced v2 versions of all five deliverables integrating the Round 4 wins: revised Principle 5 text with ownership-governance interaction, named tradition (post-1994 rules-based social-democratic), form-list for "inclusive," and explicit reference-class limitation; seven-category taxonomy (added communal-stewardship); ten-feature doctrine under specific conditions (added guarded technocratic layer, failure cases, authenticity conditions); ratchet-acknowledged capability-indexed AI package; anti-theater distributional-incidence protocol with worst-off-protection column and symmetric-adoption-and-repeal step.

**What moves forward:** the v2 deliverables feed the F1–F6 follow-up exchange menu (tracked live).

**Residual debt named by Round 5:** no external human reviewer has participated. This is the exchange's largest structural gap and is encoded as TR2 in the live roadmap.

## Key context from Exchange #7 synthesis

These findings continue to inform all downstream work and should not be forgotten even though the tasks they generated are complete:

- **Memo 01 is a public orientation artifact, not an empirical validation plan.** Do not ask it to prove recursive uplift.
- **Bounded administrative improvements alone do not reliably produce trust changes on short timelines.** The administrative-burden literature is consistent on this point; Exchange #12 corroborated for the housing domain specifically.
- **Participatory, visible reform processes have stronger evidence for trust effects than insular workflow optimization.** How the improvement happens matters more for trust than what improves.
- **The project should publicly name the evidential limits of recursive uplift.** This honesty is a strength, not a weakness.
