# Civic Blueprint — Project 2028

**An open-source blueprint for a high-capacity, human-centered society in the age of AI.**

---

## Why this exists

We are entering a period of rapid technological change, institutional strain, and growing concentration of power.

At the same time, new tools — especially in artificial intelligence — create the possibility of dramatically improving how society functions.

The question is not whether the future will change.

The question is:

**Who designs it, and who benefits from it?**

---

## What is Project 2028?

Project 2028 is an open, collaborative effort to design better systems for governance, economics, and public infrastructure.

It focuses on:

- increasing institutional capacity without sacrificing democratic accountability  
- ensuring AI expands human agency rather than concentrating power  
- reducing artificial scarcity in essential systems like housing, healthcare, and education  
- building practical, implementable frameworks — not just abstract ideas  

This is not a manifesto for a perfect world.

It is a working blueprint for building a better one.

---

## What we’re building

- **Principles**  
  A clear foundation for how systems should serve people  

- **Policy frameworks**  
  Practical approaches to real-world problems  

- **Operational playbooks**  
  How these ideas can actually be implemented  

- **Public-interest technology concepts**  
  Especially in AI, infrastructure, and coordination  

- **A network of contributors**  
  People willing to build, test, and eventually operate these systems  

---

## How to contribute

This project is being built in the open.

You can contribute by:

- proposing ideas or improvements  
- challenging assumptions and identifying risks  
- helping translate concepts into real systems  
- contributing research, writing, or technical work  

Contribution guidelines and templates are coming soon.
