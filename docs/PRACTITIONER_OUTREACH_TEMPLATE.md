# Practitioner Outreach Template

> **Use case:** Recommendation 2 outreach to invited practitioners.
>
> **Goal:** Reduce effort friction, clarify project intent, and separate entry-trust feedback from substantive framework critique.

## Short outreach template (first send)

Hi [Name] — this is a quick ask if you have capacity.

I am working on an open civic-systems project called Civic Blueprint. The writing is developed through human-AI collaboration, and I review, curate, and approve what is published.

I am not asking you to evaluate everything. I am trying to test two specific things:

1. Does the entry point feel clear and credible enough to engage with?
2. If you do read, does the framework name bottlenecks you actually recognize from practice?

Here is the memo entry point: https://civicblueprint.org/#memo

No pressure if you are busy. If your reaction is "this is confusing" or "this doesn't land," that is useful feedback too.

---

## Two-pass feedback prompt

### Pass 1: Entry-trust and legibility

- Before evaluating substance, does this make clear what the project is trying to do?
- Does the provenance framing feel honest and legible?
- What (if anything) triggers skepticism at first contact?

### Pass 2: Substantive framework critique

- (a) Does this framework name bottlenecks you recognize from practice?
- (b) What does it miss or get wrong?
- (c) Where does the cross-domain comparison help and where does it feel forced?
- (d) Would you use or recommend this analysis?

## Follow-up template for previously contacted practitioners

Hi [Name] — quick follow-up on the note I sent.

I realized the original link drops directly into the memo section and can skip orientation context. That is on me.

Short context before you look again: this is an open project on why critical systems fail and how they might be redesigned. The work is developed through human-AI collaboration, with steward review and curation before publication.

If useful, I would love your reaction on two things:

1. Is the entry point clear/credible enough to continue reading?
2. If yes, where does the framework match or miss your real-world experience?

If your response is simply "this still does not land," that is valuable signal.
